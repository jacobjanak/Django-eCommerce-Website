Monika Vitek was born and raised in the Czech Republic and now lives with her husband and daughter in Portland, Oregon. She began working in ceramics in 2009.

Monika’s work is grounded in both functionality and design, and she enjoys making pieces for everyday use that are pleasing to the eye. She works in small batches, creating each one-of-a-kind piece by hand in her home studio using a potter’s wheel and hand building techniques. She uses primarily porcelain, and frequently integrates ilmenite, a mineral that she collects at the Oregon coast. Monika appreciates the tangible nature of ceramics, and takes inspiration from the beauty of the nature and everyday life.

Coursework:
2009-2016: Portland Community College, with Richard Brandt
2016: Oregon College of Arts and Crafts, with J Shiloh Gastello

Shows/Exhibitions:
2016: Companion Gallery, juried show “Irons in the Fire”
2016: Behind the Museum Cafe
